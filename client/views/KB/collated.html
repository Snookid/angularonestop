How to deal with Minor and Major Violations
Email Correspondence
Minor Editorial Policy Violation

Action:

  • First email consisting of the violation and the suggested resolution.
  • If the client resubmits without changes, start the survey.
Major Editorial & Content Policy Violation

Action:

  • First email consisting of the violation and the suggested resolution.
  • Second email restating the violation and the suggested resolution plus the following message:
  • Non-Googler surveys

    Unfortunately, your survey will be unable to start if it is not compliant with our program policies. We will issue you a full refund should you decide to cancel/delete your survey. Please let us know which action you would like to take.

    We appreciate an opportunity to better serve you in the future and apologize for any inconvenience this has caused.

  • Googler surveys

    Unfortunately, your survey will be unable to start if it is not compliant with our program policies. Please make the necessary adjustments.

    We appreciate an opportunity to better serve you in the future and apologize for any inconvenience this has caused.

  • Third email client resubmits without edits (specially for MAJOR violations): cs pref
  • Issue a refund if the survey was purchased via prepaid.
  • Issue a coupon if the survey was purchased via coupon.
  • Issue a refund and coupon if the survey is purchased via prepaid and coupon.
  • Note: For Googlers and surveys with Screening questions (no billing), we don't need to issue a coupon or refund, just send an email.
  • Replace the subject line with (A message from Google Consumer Surveys) since the original subject line has the ACTION REQUIRED phrase which is inappropriate to include.

    SUPERLATIVE RULE

    Important: (3/2/16 transfered here)
  • Any superlative in a hypothetical question (if, when) or regarding possibility (most likely) or liking/inclination (like the most/best) can be started, even if the choices are limited.
  • Superlatives regarding FACTS would need an opt-out. The superlative in the cigarette question was predefined by a factual situation which can't be fact for everyone.

    • Example:

    >What do you like the most in your office? (opt-out needed, not everyone works in an office)

    >What do you like the most if you are/were working in an office? (hypothetical, no opt-out needed)

    >When walking past a restaurant, what attracts you most to walk in and check it out? (hypothetical, no opt-out needed)

    When to suggest Opt Outs for superlatives such as BEST, MOST, WORST, & FAVORITE:
  • For answer choices that are of usage in a logical order, you can either suggest an opt out OR edit one of the answer choices that can serve as an opt out.

    We don’t allow surveys with content that could be considered repulsive, violent, gruesome, shocking or disturbing to users.

    Notes

  • "This won't be allowed on the Pub network. Android GOR Panel is OK, you can advise them to resubmit and start on GOR" - Chi
    • 2. Gruesome subjects - Bowel movement (updated 12/8/15) "Bowel movement" is accepted as Not/Non-gruesome. However, this would still be treated on a case-to-case basis depending on the content of the survey. For bowel movement surveys, ensure that there are no gross items included in the survey and check if it is for medical purposes. If for medical purposes, include a permission screen. Note: Please asses if the survey is adult enough. Most birth control (contraceptive) surveys are adult. If unsure, you can always escalate. 3. Medical Preference Surveys - Surveys related to Medical or Surgical procedures, history or preference (updated 9/29/15)
  • For medical, only those that can be considered adult/sensitive should be on GOR + permission. Other surveys like asking for hair loss, colds, headache etc. can be on pub network + permission w/o escalating it. (7/1/2016)
    • "If there is a need those types of surveys (medical - Adult/Sensitive) should be on on the pub network, then those should be escalated and reviewed on a case by case basis." - chi
